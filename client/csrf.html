Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF) is a security vulnerability that tricks a user into performing actions they did not intend to by exploiting their authenticated session.

Why It's Dangerous: CSRF can force users to perform actions like transferring funds, changing account details, or making purchases without their consent.

How to Protect Your Application:
Use Anti-CSRF Tokens: Include unique tokens in forms and verify them on the server side.
Validate Referer Header: Check the referer header to ensure requests are coming from trusted sources.

Relation to C: In C programming, if someone had a pointer to your identity (e.g., a pointer to a sensitive data structure), they could potentially manipulate or access your data without your consent. Similarly, CSRF allows attackers to perform actions on behalf of a user without their knowledge.
